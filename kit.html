K.I.T.

Kingdom Initiative
Technologies.

Software and tools to disciple, equip, and connect the Church — built with the same engineering standards as anything commercial, but aimed at souls instead of pipelines.

The vision

Technology bent toward the Church.

The enemy has used technology to disciple a generation into anxiety, distraction, and counterfeit identity. The Church doesn't need to hide from the tools — she needs saints willing to rebuild them, on purpose, under Christ.

K.I.T. is my attempt at that. A slow, prayerful body of work — building real systems that serve real believers, rooted in Scripture and built to last. Every tool points back to the local church. Every line of code is stewardship.

"For God is not a God of disorder but of peace." — 1 Corinthians 14:40

What's being built

Two tools. Active development.

These are the first confirmed projects under K.I.T. — built in obedience, tested in real life, and designed to serve the Body of Christ.

Disciple

WisdomWatch

A personal discipleship journal powered by AI. Scripture reading plans, prayer tracking, daily examen, and an intelligent companion trained on orthodox Christian theology — designed to help believers grow in wisdom, not just consume content.

Built to disappear into real life, not replace it.

Equip

K.C.R.M.

Kingdom Church Relationship Management — lightweight software for pastors, ministry leaders, and missionaries who can't afford an IT team. Member care, follow-up systems, discipleship pathways, and communication tools — all built for the way churches actually work.

Because shepherds shouldn't need a tech degree to care for their flock.

The bigger vision

Where this goes if God confirms it.

These are not products yet. They are possible obedience points — directions I'm praying over. Whatever the Lord confirms, that's what gets built.

Connect

Nexus

A platform to connect believers worldwide — anti-scroll, anti-clout, pro-presence. Technology that pushes people off the screen and into each other's living rooms.

Intelligence

N.I.A.

An AI system trained exclusively for Kingdom work — theology, pastoral care, discipleship guidance. A tool for church leaders, not a replacement for them.

Scale

White-label

K.I.T. systems adapted for military, schools, missions organizations — anywhere discipleship infrastructure is needed but doesn't exist yet.

Guardrails

What K.I.T. will never be.

  • An attention farm. No infinite scroll. No streaks engineered to exploit.
  • A personality cult. The founder is not the point.
  • A theological free-for-all. Orthodox, Christ-centered, Scripture-rooted.
  • A VC rocketship. Slow, funded by real work, free to stay small.
  • A substitute for the local church. Every tool points back to her.
  • Built in secret. You'll see every brick laid in public.
