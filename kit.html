K.I.T.

Kingdom Initiative
Technologies.

Software and tools to disciple, equip, and connect the Church. In the listening phase — not the launch phase.

The vision

Technology bent toward the Church.

The enemy has used technology to disciple a generation into anxiety, distraction, and counterfeit identity. The Church doesn't need to hide from the tools — she needs saints willing to rebuild them, on purpose, under Christ.

K.I.T. is my attempt at that. Not another Christian app. A slow, prayerful body of work — built with the same standards as anything shipped at Apexera, but aimed at souls instead of pipelines.

What it could become

Three directions I'm praying over.

These are not products. They are possible obedience points. Whatever the Lord confirms, that's what gets built.

Disciple

Tools for daily formation.

Scripture, prayer, examen, accountability — simple rhythms, not a dopamine app. Built to disappear into real life, not replace it.

Equip

Tools for local church workers.

Lightweight software for pastors, ministry leaders, and missionaries who can't afford an IT team — and who were never meant to be one.

Connect

Tools for real community.

Anti-scroll. Anti-clout. Pro-presence. Technology that pushes believers off the screen and into each other's living rooms.

Guardrails

What K.I.T. will never be.

  • An attention farm. No infinite scroll. No streaks engineered to exploit.
  • A personality cult. The founder is not the point.
  • A theological free-for-all. Orthodox, Christ-centered, Scripture-rooted.
  • A VC rocketship. Slow, funded by real work, free to stay small.
  • A substitute for the local church. Every tool points back to her.
  • Built in secret. You'll see every brick — in the Journal.
"Unless the Lord builds the house, the builders labor in vain." — Psalm 127:1. K.I.T. waits on that verse. If it never ships, it was never His.
